About This Project

Research Overview

This project examines county-level voter turnout inequality across five Midwest states — Illinois, Indiana, Ohio, Wisconsin, and Michigan — using a predictive modeling approach to identify counties that perform above or below their structural expectations. The analysis pairs quantitative modeling with qualitative case-study investigation to generate hypotheses about the civic, institutional, and historical factors that drive participation beyond what demographics predict.

The work builds on practicum research conducted with the Chicago Lawyers’ Committee for Civil Rights (CLC), which uses quantitative analysis of turnout data to inform voting rights advocacy across the region.

Methods Summary

Methodology

Turnout rates are calculated using Citizen Voting Age Population (CVAP) denominators from ACS Special Tabulation estimates. CVAP is the appropriate denominator for voting rights research because it accounts for the legally eligible electorate rather than all residents or all adults.

Structural predictors are drawn from the 2020 ACS 5-Year Estimates and retrieved via the tidycensus R package. The five predictors — educational attainment, internet access, limited English proficiency, disability status, and poverty — represent well-documented correlates of voter participation in the political science and public administration literature.

Case selection follows a purposive residual-based logic: two counties from the top of the residual distribution (over-performers), two near zero (near-expected), and two from the bottom (under-performers). Selection was also guided by geographic diversity across states.
