Research Design

What This Project Does

Existing research on voter turnout tends to focus on individual-level determinants — income, education, age — drawing on survey data. County-level analysis offers a complementary lens: it captures the aggregate civic environment in which individuals make participation decisions, and it surfaces variation that individual models can obscure.

This project builds an OLS regression model predicting county-level turnout rates in the 2020 general election across Illinois, Indiana, Ohio, Wisconsin, and Michigan. The model includes five structural predictors — educational attainment, internet access, limited English proficiency, disability rates, and poverty — drawn from the American Community Survey. Residuals from this model identify counties that perform better or worse than their structural conditions predict.

Those residuals are the core analytic object. The counties with the largest positive residuals are “over-performers”: something beyond their demographics is driving participation. The counties with the largest negative residuals are “under-performers”: something is suppressing turnout that the model can’t explain on its own. The counties closest to zero are the “expected” cases.

Why Residuals? Turnout rates alone can be misleading. A county with 65% turnout might be underperforming if its demographics predict 72%, while a county with 58% might be over-performing given its structural constraints. Residual analysis shifts the question from how much to relative to what.

Data & Methods

Data Sources

ACS 5-Year CVAP Denominators State Election Commissions

Turnout rates are calculated using Citizen Voting Age Population (CVAP) denominators from ACS estimates, consistent with best practices in voting rights research. Using CVAP rather than total population or VAP ensures the denominator reflects the legally eligible electorate.

Structural predictors are drawn from the 2020 ACS 5-Year estimates:

Predictor ACS Table Rationale
% Bachelor’s degree or higher B15003 Civic engagement, information access
% Households with internet access B28002 Voter registration, information environment
% Limited English proficiency B16004 Ballot access barriers
% With a disability B18101 Participation barriers, registration gaps
% Below poverty line B17001 Resource constraints on participation

All data were retrieved using the tidycensus R package. County GEOID codes are used as the merge key across all datasets.
